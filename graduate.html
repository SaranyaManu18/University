STEP 1: Academic Criteria

Applicants for admission to Sunray University graduate programs must hold a bachelor’s degree from an accredited American college or university, or the equivalent degree from a college or university outside the United States. If you are in the process of completing your bachelor’s degree, you may still apply and your offer of admission would be contingent upon completion of the bachelor's degree. Previous college work is typically expected to be a 3.0 grade point average or higher and should reflect advanced course study. In addition, applicants must provide the admission requirements specific to the program. Please refer to each program site for details on these requirements, as well as application deadlines.

International Criteria

Individuals whose native language is not English must include official scores from the Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL). Information concerning TOEFL may be obtained by writing: TOEFL, Educational Testing Service, Princeton, NJ 08541. Request that the results are sent to Sarah Lawrence College, ETS code 2810. For more information: TOEFL.

ADMISSION CRITERIA

Admission to Sunray University is competitive.The Sunray University strongly encourages a college preparatory curriculum in high school. Students are also advised to apply early.

  • Your academic performance in high school
  • High School GPA of 3.25 or higher
  • The strength and quality of your curriculum
  • Your ACT or SAT results
  • Your talents, leadership, conduct and diversity of experience
